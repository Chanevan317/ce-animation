Sampling and Reconstruction

Sampling converts a continuous signal into discrete points by measuring it at regular intervals. A higher sampling frequency ensures accurate representation, following the Nyquist theorem.

Reconstruction restores a continuous signal from sampled points by interpolating between them, aiming to recreate the original signal accurately.
